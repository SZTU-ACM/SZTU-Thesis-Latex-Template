\keywordsen{Shenzhen Technology University; LaTeX; undergraduate thesis template}
\categoryen{TP391}
\begin{abstracten}
With a well-designed \LaTeX{} template, the thesis can be compiled into a PDF with uniform formatting. At present, most domestic publishers and universities still rely on Microsoft Word. Because of Word's flexibility, newcomers who must follow a rigid format often find that typesetting, numbering, and bibliographies become unexpectedly time-consuming; achieving the efficiency of a full-document layout change in \LaTeX{} typically requires advanced Word skills.

To let authors focus on writing, many international journals and universities support \LaTeX{} for preparation and submission. Beginners need only follow a small set of structural commands to obtain a compliant document. When global layout changes are required, editing or replacing the template file and recompiling quickly yields an updated PDF---a workflow that is difficult to match for inexperienced Word users.

This project aims to provide a \LaTeX{} template that follows the writing norms for undergraduate graduation theses (designs) at Shenzhen Technology University, and to reduce the burden of repeated manual formatting.
\end{abstracten}
